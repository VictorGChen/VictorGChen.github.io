Hi, my name is Victor Chen, a bioinformatician interested in the fields of immune response, machine learning, and population genomics. I have experience developing and testing analysis pipelines for both research and industrial use.

With over 5 years of Python experience, I've developed a professional interest in clinical diagnostics and personalized healthcare. My work spans from building production diagnostic algorithms at Kelliop Inc. to conducting cutting-edge research in bioinformatics, always with a focus on translating complex biological data into actionable insights that can improve patient outcomes.

Currently, I'm working on exposome data analysis at Stanford University's Snyder Lab, investigating how environmental and lifestyle factors influence cytokine responses. I'm also exploring single-cell sequencing analysis to understand cellular heterogeneity in immune responses, combining trajectory analysis with dimensionality reduction techniques to uncover time- and dose-dependent patterns in biological systems.

The integration of multi-omics data with machine learning represents an exciting frontier in personalized healthcare. I believe these technologies can democratize access to precision medicine by making complex diagnostic tools more affordable and accessible. By developing scalable bioinformatics pipelines and user-friendly platforms, we can bring the power of genomic and proteomic insights to a broader patient population, ultimately improving health outcomes across diverse communities.

The current biological problem that I am most interested in is how inflammatory resolution differences between individuals can differ based on the factors of stress, sleep, and utilization of inflamed areas.
